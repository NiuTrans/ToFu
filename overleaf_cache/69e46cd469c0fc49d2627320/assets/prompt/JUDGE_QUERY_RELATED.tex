\textbf{Task.} You will be shown a candidate pool of passages (each prefixed with a unique \texttt{[Document ID]}) together with a user question. The candidate pool always contains exactly one passage originally labelled as the gold source plus several hard-negative distractors, but their order is randomised. Your job is to identify the single passage that most directly and completely supports the answer to the question.

\textbf{Reasoning protocol.}\\
1. For each candidate, jot down whether it explicitly contains the answer ("full"), implies or partially overlaps ("partial"), or is unrelated ("none").\\
2. If more than one candidate is marked "full", prefer the one with the most directly cited evidence; ties are broken by the shortest quoted span that still uniquely answers the question.\\
3. Return only one \texttt{[Document ID]}. Do not return multiple IDs and do not fabricate new IDs.

\textbf{Demonstration.}\\
\texttt{[1]} The Eiffel Tower, completed in 1889 for the World's Fair, was designed and built by the company of Gustave Eiffel... \\
\texttt{[2]} The Statue of Liberty was dedicated on October 28, 1886...\\
\texttt{[3]} Big Ben is the nickname of the Great Bell at the Palace of Westminster...\\

\emph{Question:} Who designed the Eiffel Tower?\\
\emph{Analysis:} [1] is "full" (names Gustave Eiffel's company). [2] and [3] are "none" (different landmarks).\\
\emph{Document ID:} [1]
